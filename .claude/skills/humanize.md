Trigger: when the user asks to humanize, clean up, or de-AI writing — or before any client-facing content is finalized.

Strip AI tells from a draft so it reads like a real person wrote it.

---

## Process

Run two passes, then an audit.

**Pass 1 — Voice.** Add what AI omits by default:
- An actual opinion or reaction, not just description
- Rhythm variation: mix short sentences with longer ones
- First person where it fits
- A specific detail in place of an abstraction
- Mixed feelings where the topic is genuinely mixed

**Pass 2 — Tells.** Scan for the patterns below and rewrite them.

**Audit.** Ask: "What would still tip a reader off that this is AI?" Answer in 1–2 lines. Revise once more based on your answer.

---

## Tells to Cut or Rewrite

**Family 1 — Inflated importance**
Words to cut: *stands as, testament, pivotal, underscores, reflects broader, evolving landscape, key turning point, seamless, breathtaking, renowned, unlock, empower, vibrant, robust, holistic, compelling, multifaceted, paradigm-shifting, delve into, tapestry, realm, harness, foster, unveil, showcase, crucial.*

Vague attribution ("experts argue", "studies show") without a name or citation is filler — remove or replace with something specific.

**Family 2 — Fake depth**
-ing endings doing no real work: *highlighting, emphasizing, ensuring, fostering, reflecting, contributing, reinforcing* — rewrite as direct statements.
- "Serves as" / "plays a role" — replace with plain "is".
- Rule of three used as padding — cut to two when the third adds nothing.
- "Not just X but also Y" — overused. Pick one or rewrite.

**Family 3 — Cosmetic tells**
- Em dashes everywhere — use commas or periods instead.
- Bold for no reason — if it's not a header or critical warning, remove it.
- Title Case Headings — use sentence case.
- Emojis — remove unless explicitly requested.
- Inline-header lists ("Speed: faster load times.") — rewrite as prose.
- False ranges ("everything from X to Y") — almost always overstated, be specific.

**Family 4 — Conversational artifacts**
- Chatbot framing: "Here's a breakdown… Let me know if you'd like me to expand!" — cut, just answer.
- Knowledge-cutoff hedges: "While details are limited…" — remove or state the fact directly.
- Sycophancy: "Great question!" — delete.
- Filler phrases: "In order to", "has the ability to", "it should be noted that" — cut.
- Excessive hedging: "may potentially", "could possibly" — pick one qualifier or none.
- Generic conclusions: "Overall, the outlook is positive…" — replace with a specific next action or fact.

---

## Output Format

1. **Rewrite** — both passes applied
2. **Audit** — 1–2 sentences: what still reads as AI and why
3. **Final rewrite** — revised based on the audit
